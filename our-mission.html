E-Waste: The Growing Crisis

E-waste is the "elephant in the room" that we’ve long ignored.
  • India is the 3rd largest producer of e-waste, generating over 2 million metric tons of waste annually.
  • Most e-waste is managed by the unorganised sector , posing severe environmental and health risks.

    • It’s time to take action and address the hazards of e-waste before it leads to long-term damage.

Here’s why e-waste demands immediate attention
  • 3.2 million metric tons of e-waste is produced every day in India.
  • 70% of this waste comes from the private sector, while individual households contribute 16%
  • Hazardous materials in e-waste (such as lead, mercury, and cadmium) pose a serious risk to public health and the environment if not handled properly.

The Private Sector's Role in E-Waste Management

With 70% of India’s e-waste generated by the private sector, companies play a crucial role in reducing the environmental impact. Without responsible e-waste management, public health risks will continue to rise.

At E2E, we help companies take charge of their e-waste and ensure it’s managed sustainably, reducing risks to both the environment and public health.

E-waste problem in India

Despite being the 3rd largest producer of electronic waste, India still lacks a systematic e-waste management plan. 95% of e-waste in India is handled by illegal sectors and they resort to harmful methodologies to deal with e-waste. They either burn them or stack it up in landfills, both of these methods pose a serious threat to the environment. E-waste contains harmful elements like Mercury, Lead, and PVC. Burning them will pollute the environment and can lead to respiratory issues among people living in that area. If this waste is left untreated in landfills, then it will seep into the groundwater and contaminate the whole ecosystem.
